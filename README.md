## The thread connecting my work

Most data science work lives or dies on one question: *how confident should we be in this estimate, and what's the cost of being wrong?*

That question looks different in every domain — in options pricing it's volatility uncertainty, in credit risk it's default probability, in sports analytics it's regression to the mean. My MS Statistics background means I approach all of them from the same first principles: understand the data generating process, quantify uncertainty honestly, and communicate the result clearly.
